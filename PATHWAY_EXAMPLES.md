## üè• Pre-Medical Pathway

### Freshman Year
**Fall:**
- CHEM 161 - General Chemistry I
- CHEM 161L - Lab
- MATH 140 - Calculus I
- BIOL 171 - Biology I
- ENG 100 - Composition

**Spring:**
- CHEM 162 - General Chemistry II
- CHEM 162L - Lab
- MATH 141 - Calculus II
- BIOL 172 - Biology II
- PHYS 170 - Physics I

**Activities:** Pre-Health Club, volunteer at hospital
**Shadowing:** Begin physician shadowing (50+ hours)

### Sophomore Year
**Fall:**
- CHEM 272 - Organic Chemistry I
- CHEM 272L - Lab
- PHYS 272 - Physics II
- BIOL 301 - Cell Biology
- Psychology or Sociology

**Spring:**
- CHEM 273 - Organic Chemistry II
- CHEM 273L - Lab
- BIOL 310 - Genetics
- BIOL 350 - Microbiology
- GE Course

**Activities:** Research lab assistant, health education programs
**Clinical:** 100+ volunteer hours in healthcare setting

### Junior Year
**Fall:**
- BIOC 441 - Biochemistry
- BIOC 441L - Lab
- Upper-level biology elective
- GE/Electives (2)

**Spring:**
- BIOL 406 - Physiology
- ANAT 420 - Anatomy
- Medical ethics course
- GE/Elective

**Activities:** Leadership in Pre-Health Club, research presentation
**Clinical:** EMT certification, continued volunteering (200+ total hours)
**MCAT:** Begin MCAT preparation

### Senior Year
**Fall:**
- Upper-level science electives
- Medical humanities
- Complete major requirements
- MCAT exam (ideally by September)

**Spring:**
- Complete remaining coursework
- Submit medical school applications (AMCAS)
- Secondary applications
- Interview preparation

**Activities:** Research thesis/publication, leadership roles
**Clinical:** Scribe or medical assistant part-time (500+ total hours)

**Career:** Medical School ‚Üí Physician

**Spring:**

**Activities:** Healthcare facility volunteering, CPR certification
**Major:** Apply to nursing program (competitive admission)

### Junior Year (Nursing Program)
**Fall:**
- NURS 310 - Health Assessment
- NURS 320 - Fundamentals of Nursing
- NURS 330 - Pharmacology
- NURS 340 - Pathophysiology

**Spring:**

- Lab practicum

**Skills:** IV insertion, medication administration, care planning

### Senior Year

**Spring:**
